Be A Star!

Stars are the most widely recognised astronomical matter in space. They are responsible for the creation and distribution of a number of elements, such as carbon, nitrogen and oxygen. The study of a star's life cycle is also one of the most essential topics to learn within the field of astronomy.

Stars are formed inside dust clouds through turbulence, which gives rise to knots containing a large enough mass of gas and dust to collapse under its own gravitational attraction. This heats up the material at the centre, giving birth to a protostar. This protostar is the heart of the core, and will one day become a star.
